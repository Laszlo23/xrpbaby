# Legal layering and offerings (framework)

**This document is not legal advice.** Engage qualified counsel in each jurisdiction before soliciting capital or selling tokens.

## Three layers

| Layer | Entity / instrument | Purpose |
|-------|---------------------|---------|
| **Protocol company** | Operating company (equity, SAFEs, rounds) | Builds software, runs hosting, marketing, operations; earns **disclosed protocol fees**. |
| **Platform settlement token** | ERC-20 on Base (if offered) | **Checkout and routing** for protocol-integrated flows; must not be marketed as a share of real-estate cash flows unless registered or exempt. |
| **Property / SPV** | Legal entity holding title | **Rent, appreciation, sale proceeds** flow per **offering documents** (PPM, prospectus, or local equivalent); on-chain tokens represent claims only as described in those documents. |

## Disclosure checklist

1. **Separate documents** for (a) software/protocol participation, (b) any token sale, (c) each property or basket SPV.
2. **Risk factors**: illiquidity, smart contract risk, regulatory risk, property market risk, operator reliance.
3. **No merged marketing**: avoid implying that the platform token entitles holders to building rent unless that is legally true and documented.
4. **Compliance**: accredited / qualified investor rules where applicable; geoblocking where required.
5. **Treasury governance**: publish multisig addresses and high-level signing policy.

## Founder vs investor economics

- **Founders / team**: salary and equity via **company cap table**; liquidity from fundraising rounds.
- **Protocol treasury**: receives **fee revenue** per published schedule (see [economics-fee-schedule.md](./economics-fee-schedule.md)).
- **Property investors**: returns from **legal SPV waterfalls**, not from undisclosed token mints.

Keep cap-table negotiations and property waterfalls in **different** agreements.
